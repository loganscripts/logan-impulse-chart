logan’s impulse chart

what is impulse control?

impulse control means stopping yourself from doing something without thinking. it's about pausing and making a smart choice instead of doing the first thing that pops into your head.

why do we need impulse control?

without impulse control, we might hurt others, break rules, or make choices that we regret. it helps us be respectful, stay safe, and grow up to be responsible people.

the brain science behind it

impulse control lives in the prefrontal cortex — the front part of the brain. that part helps us think, plan, and pause before we act. it’s one of the last brain parts to fully develop, which is why kids and teens are still learning impulse control skills.

how does it affect us?

  • our choices (like whether we follow rules or act out)
  • our friendships (people may not trust us if we act wild)
  • our safety (impulsive actions can lead to accidents or danger)

examples of impulse control

your friend gets a new toy and won’t share. you want to grab it from them, but instead you walk away and tell a teacher. that’s impulse control.
your friends don’t let you play so you steal the ball from them. that’s not impulse control.
you’re mad and want to yell, but you take deep breaths and talk calmly. that’s impulse control.

pros and cons of impulse control

pros:

  • better relationships
  • more trust from adults
  • helps you stay safe and out of trouble
  • makes you a better problem-solver

cons (when you don’t use it):

  • fighting or arguing more
  • getting in trouble at school or home
  • hurting others or yourself
  • losing friends or respect
